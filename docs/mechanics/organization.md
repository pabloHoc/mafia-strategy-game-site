# Organization

## Purpose

Represent the identity, structure, culture, and strategic direction of the player's criminal empire.

Acts as the connective layer between crew, assets, territories, missions, and schemes.

## Core Questions

- Who are we?
- Who rules?
- What are we trying to achieve?

Any mechanic that does not primarily answer one of these questions likely belongs in another system.

## Components

### Origin
- How did we begin?
- Permanent or extremely difficult to change.
- Defines initial conditions and narrative background.

### Organization Type
- How are we structured?
- Rarely changed during play.
- Defines organizational structure and identity.

### Ethos
- Who are we?
- Represents values and culture.
- Influences internal politics and reactions.

### Doctrine
- What are we pursuing right now?
- Dynamic and changeable.
- Creates strategic direction.
- Favors friction over hard restrictions.

### Offices
- Who holds power?
- Filled by crew members.
- Subject to succession and conflict.

### Attributes
- Represent organizational state.
- Reflect health, reputation, and stability.
- Are consumed by other systems.

## System Interactions

- Crew occupy offices.
- Assets belong to organizations.
- Territories are administered by organizations.
- Missions advance organizational goals.
- Schemes emerge from ambitions.

## Design Principles

- Separate identity from strategy.
- Favor friction over hard restrictions.
- Prefer broad mechanics with multiple uses.
- Generate stories through systemic interactions.
- Focus on identity and governance.

## Design Areas

### Identity
Origin, Type, Ethos, permanence.

### Leadership
Offices, assignment, requirements, succession.

### Strategy
Doctrine, doctrine changes, Ethos-Doctrine interactions.

### State
Attributes and consumers.

### Internal Politics
Crises, rivalries, power struggles.

## Stress Test

### Character Sheet Syndrome
Organization must generate recurring decisions.

### Ethos Becoming Flavor
Ethos should create tensions and incentives.

### Doctrine Becoming Solved
Every doctrine should have tradeoffs and costs.

### Offices Becoming Busywork
Offices should create stories rather than modifiers.

### Internal Politics Consuming the Game
Politics should complicate the game, not replace it.

### Excessive Permanence
Identity should be stable while strategy remains flexible.

The organization should answer:
- Who are we?
- What are we becoming?

## Strategic Tradeoffs

Organizational choices should create competing pressures:

- growth vs stability
- secrecy vs expansion
- loyalty vs efficiency
- influence vs fear
- short-term survival vs long-term goals

Every advantage should create a vulnerability.

## Gameplay Loop

Identity → Strategy → Consequences → Organizational State → Leadership Pressure → New Strategy

## Anti-Bloat Rule

If a mechanic does not answer:

- Who are we?
- Who rules?
- What are we trying to achieve?

it likely belongs elsewhere.
