
# Inspirations

This document collects games and systems that inspire specific aspects of the design.

The goal is not to replicate these games, but to understand and adapt their strengths.

## Organizations & Crew Management

### Darkest Dungeon

- Roster management
- Character specialization and synergies
- Mission preparation and provisioning
- Stress and long-term consequences

### Blades in the Dark

- Crew progression
- Heat and wanted levels
- Stress and trauma
- Downtime actions
- Factions and claims
- Progress clocks
- Mission structure

### Terra Invicta

- Agents and missions
- Faction ideology and alignment
- Global operations
- Control points

### Shadow of the Forbidden Gods

- Agents operating in the world
- Infiltration and hidden influence
- Asymmetric playstyles

## Politics, Intrigue & Leverage

### Crusader Kings III

- Hooks and leverage
- Schemes and intrigue
- Character-driven politics

### Undying

- Debts and social obligations
- Political maneuvering
- Means, plots, and schemes

### Urban Shadows

- Debts as social currency
- Faction relationships
- Urban supernatural politics

## Missions & Asset Commitment

### Cultist Simulator

- Cards as resources and requirements
- Narrative progression through combinations
- Hidden knowledge and discovery

### Fallen London / Sunless Sea / Sunless Skies

- Opportunities as narrative resources
- Cards and storylets
- Requirements and consequences

### Rebuild 3

- Assigning characters to missions
- City-scale strategy
- Real-time task resolution

## Information & Espionage

### Stellaris

- Espionage systems
- Information asymmetry
- Intelligence gathering

### Shadow of the Forbidden Gods

- Hidden operations
- Gradual infiltration
- Secrecy as strategy
