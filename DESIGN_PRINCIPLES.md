# Design Principles

## Vision

- Create a strategy game about building and leading a hidden organization in a dark urban fantasy city, where power comes not from territorial conquest but from leverage, relationships, and ambition.
- The game should emphasize long-term planning, emergent stories, and difficult tradeoffs between growth, loyalty, and influence.

## Player Fantasy

- The player is the leader of a clandestine organization navigating a hidden struggle for power.
- Through trusted lieutenants, valuable assets, and political maneuvering, they grow from a small operation into a shadow power capable of reshaping the city.
- The fantasy is not to conquer territory, but to build an enduring organization.

---

## Design Principles

### Assets Are Leverage

- Assets represent the ability to influence the world, not merely possessions or resources.
- Examples include favors, secrets, organizations, businesses, and people.
- Power is not what you own, but what you can leverage.

### Organizations Scale Power

- The primary form of progression is organizational growth.
- Players expand their influence by building structures of trust, delegation, and hierarchy rather than accumulating raw resources.

### Territory Is Opportunity

- Districts are not owned provinces.
- They are evolving opportunity spaces that provide missions, relationships, and strategic advantages.

### Missions Are Commitments

- Missions are decisions to commit assets under uncertainty.
- Choosing one opportunity means forgoing another.

### Politics Create Stories

- Internal and external politics should generate emergent narratives.
- Ambition, loyalty, rivalry, and succession are features, not failures.

### Information Is Power

- Information is valuable, incomplete, and contested.
- Players should rarely possess perfect knowledge of rivals or the world.

### Growth Creates Risk

- Expansion should introduce new challenges.
- Larger organizations become more powerful, but also harder to manage and more vulnerable to internal conflict.

### Depth Through Interactions

- Complexity should emerge from interactions between systems rather than from isolated mechanics.
- Favor systemic design over bespoke rules.

## Adding New Mechanics

When introducing a new mechanic, consider the following questions.

### Generic Design Questions

#### Decisions & Tradeoffs

- Does it create meaningful decisions?
- Does choosing one option require sacrificing another?
- Does it introduce risk, uncertainty, or opportunity cost?

#### Complexity

- Does it add strategic depth rather than complexity for its own sake?
- Can the mechanic be explained simply?
- Does it minimize bookkeeping and micromanagement?

#### Systemic Interactions

- Does it interact with multiple existing systems?
- Does it create new synergies or tensions?
- Can it produce emergent outcomes?

#### Reusability

- Can this mechanic be reused across multiple systems or contexts?
- Does it serve more than one purpose?
- Does it operate on multiple layers of gameplay?

#### Progression & Scaling

- How does this mechanic evolve throughout the game?
- Does it remain relevant in both early and late game?
- Does it scale through organizational growth rather than numerical inflation?

#### Failure Modes

- Could this mechanic become repetitive?
- Could it become dominant or mandatory?
- Does it risk creating degenerate strategies?

### Project-Specific Questions

#### Organizational Impact

- Does it reinforce the fantasy of building and managing an organization?
- Does it create relationships between people, assets, and institutions?
- Does it encourage delegation, hierarchy, or internal politics?

#### Information & Uncertainty

- Does it create or leverage imperfect information?
- Does it generate opportunities for intelligence, secrets, or deception?

#### Pressure & Ambition

- Why would the player engage with this mechanic?
- Does it encourage action rather than passive optimization?
- Does it create incentives to expand, take risks, or pursue ambitions?

#### Story Generation

- What stories does this mechanic create?
- Can it generate rivalries, betrayals, crises, or unexpected outcomes?

### Final Question

- If this mechanic were removed, would the game become less interesting?
