# Missions × Assets × Institutions — Verb-Based System

**Status:** Core Design Exploration (Checkpoint)

---

# Core Idea

The game is structured around a shared **verb language system** that connects:

- **Missions (problems)**
- **Infrastructure Assets (spatial leverage)**
- **Leverage Assets (situational power)**
- **Institutions (system rules / resistance layer)**

Instead of matching assets directly to missions, everything is expressed through a **common verb vocabulary**.

---

# 1. System Layers

## Infrastructure Assets (Spatial Layer)

Persistent, district-based structures that provide **location-based capabilities**.

### Core verbs:
- Hide
- Route
- Stage
- Harbor
- Channel
- Produce
- Host
- Secure

### Mental model:
> “What can I do here because I control this place?”

### Role:
- Enable repeated actions in a district
- Shape geography of operations
- Create local opportunities

---

## Leverage Assets (Transactional Layer)

Consumable or situational power used to **force immediate outcomes**.

### Core verbs:
- Bribe
- Force
- Cancel
- Silence
- Shield
- Expose
- Extract
- Threaten

### Mental model:
> “What can I make happen right now?”

### Role:
- Solve immediate mission problems
- Override resistance temporarily
- Get consumed or exhausted when used

---

## Institutions (System Layer)

Global rule systems that define **baseline resistance and permissions**.

### Core verbs:
- Enforce
- Investigate
- Restrict
- Permit
- Validate
- Obstruct
- Delay
- Legitimize

### Mental model:
> “How does the world normally behave against me?”

### Role:
- Define default difficulty and constraints
- Can be infiltrated or influenced
- Modify systemic conditions (not directly used in missions)

---

# 2. Mission Structure

Missions are defined as **structured problems**, not scripted sequences.

Each mission contains:

## A) Core Objective
What must be achieved.

Examples:
- Extract target
- Eliminate target
- Smuggle cargo
- Sabotage facility
- Control district outcome

---

## B) Pressure Conditions
What makes the mission difficult.

Examples:
- High security presence
- Active investigation
- Limited time window
- Surveillance coverage
- Rival interference

---

## C) Complication Slots
Optional additional constraints.

Examples:
- Witnesses present
- Unknown layout
- Evidence sensitivity
- Internal betrayal risk

---

# 3. Key Bridge: Verb Requirements

Missions do NOT reference assets.

They require **verb outcomes under pressure conditions**.

Example:

## Prison Break Mission

### Objective:
- Extract prisoner

### Pressure:
- High security surveillance
- Fast response time

### Required verbs:
- Hide
- Route
- Extract

---

# 4. Resolution Model

Players solve missions by combining verbs from different layers:

### Infrastructure provides:
- Hide, Route, Stage, Harbor

### Leverage provides:
- Cancel, Shield, Silence, Force

### Institutions modify:
- Enforce, Permit, Restrict (as baseline system pressure)

---

# 5. Core Design Principles

## 1. No asset-to-mission direct mapping
Assets never directly “apply to missions”.

They only provide verbs.

---

## 2. Missions never reference assets
They only define:
- Objectives
- Pressures
- Required verbs

---

## 3. Institutions are not assets
They define systemic behavior, not player-owned resources.

---

## 4. Verbs are the universal interface
All systems communicate through a shared verb vocabulary.

---

# 6. Key Benefits

- Removes asset-mission compatibility explosion
- Preserves strong asset identity
- Enables cross-system interaction without coupling
- Makes design scalable through a controlled verb set
- Creates clear player mental model:
  > “What actions do I need to assemble?”

---

# 7. Critical Constraints

- Verb list must remain tightly controlled (≈15–30 core verbs)
- Assets cannot freely invent new verbs
- Missions must not bypass verb system
- Institutions must remain systemic, not consumable

---

# 8. Open Questions

- What is the final canonical verb list?
- How many verbs are too many before complexity explodes?
- Can verbs evolve or unlock over time?
- How are conflicting verbs resolved in missions?
- Do some verbs require multiple layers to succeed (e.g. Hide + Shield)?

---

# 9. Stress Test Targets

- Define 10 missions and ensure all can be expressed via verbs
- Define 15 infrastructure assets and verify uniqueness of verb sets
- Ensure no verb duplicates identical function across layers
- Validate that removing one asset changes strategy meaningfully
