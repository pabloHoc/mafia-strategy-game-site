# Missions

**Status:** WIP

## Purpose

Missions are the active operational layer of the game.

They transform resources and strategic position into concrete outcomes, generating conflict, progression, and emergent stories.

## Core Concept

Missions are short and medium-term operations executed by organizations.

They consume resources, create opportunities, and drive interaction between factions.

Design principle:

> Missions are the central gameplay loop of the game.

## Entities

- Mission
- Organization
- Lieutenant
- Crew
- Asset
- Territory
- Intel

## Structure

Every mission defines:

- Objective
- Requirements
- Duration
- Risk
- Rewards

Longer missions generally provide greater rewards but increased exposure.

## Categories

Mission categories provide structure and specialization.

- Economic
- Intrigue
- Criminal
- Exploration
- Expansion
- Plot

## Availability

Missions may be:

- Generated by territories
- Created by organizations
- Discovered through Intel
- Triggered by events
- Unlocked by Schemes

Not all missions are visible to all factions.

## Assignment

Missions require committing resources.

Committed resources become unavailable until the mission resolves.

Typical resources include:

- Lieutenants
- Crew
- Assets
- Wealth
- Intel

Design principle:

> Choosing one opportunity means abandoning another.

## Intel

Intel reveals information about missions before execution.

More Intel reduces uncertainty but consumes time and resources.

Design principle:

> Information is power, but delay has costs.

## Approaches

A mission may support multiple approaches.

Approaches alter:

- Requirements
- Risk
- Rewards
- Consequences

Design goal:

> Multiply strategic possibilities without multiplying content.

## Mission Interaction

Missions may interact with one another through:

- Opposition
- Parallel objectives
- Sabotage
- Counter-missions

Design goal:

> Increase player interaction without requiring direct warfare.

## Progression

Missions may unlock additional missions or contribute to Schemes.

Missions are short and medium-term actions.

Schemes are long-term goals composed of multiple missions.

## Consequences

Missions leave lasting traces in the world.

Consequences may affect:

- Heat
- Faction relationships
- Territory state
- Future opportunities

Design principles:

> The world learns from the player.
>
> The city remembers.

## Design Notes

Design principles:

- Generate meaningful choices every turn.
- Connect multiple systems together.
- Encourage specialization and long-term planning.
- Produce conflict and counterplay.
- Generate stories naturally through success and failure.
- Prefer emergent systems over bespoke mechanics.
