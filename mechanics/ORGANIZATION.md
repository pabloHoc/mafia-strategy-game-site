# Organization

**Status:** WIP

## Purpose

Represent the identity, structure, culture, and strategic direction of the player's criminal empire.

Provide the connective layer between people, assets, territories, missions, and schemes while reinforcing the fantasy of building an enduring organization.

## Core Concept

Organizations are structures of power rather than collections of resources. They define who the player is, who holds power, and what the organization is currently trying to achieve.

The mechanic separates long-term identity from short-term strategy. Identity should remain relatively stable, while strategy adapts to changing circumstances and creates meaningful tradeoffs.

Growth should create both power and risk. Larger organizations become more capable through delegation and specialization, but also harder to manage and more vulnerable to internal instability.

## Rules

### Identity

Organizations possess long-term identity components that define their history, structure, and values.

Identity choices should be difficult or impossible to change.

### Leadership

Organizations contain offices occupied by crew members.

Offices connect characters to organizational power and may be affected by succession, promotion, and conflict.

### Strategy

Organizations adopt doctrines representing current priorities and goals.

Doctrine should be dynamic and create meaningful tradeoffs.

### Organizational State

Organizations possess attributes that continuously change in response to player actions and world events.

Attributes are shared resources used by multiple systems.

### Internal Politics

Organizations can generate internal tensions and conflicts as a consequence of growth and leadership.

Internal politics should complicate the game rather than replace it.

### Dynamics

Organizations evolve through changes in:

- leadership
- strategy
- organizational state
- internal stability

Changes in one area should create consequences in others.

## Player Interaction & Tradeoffs

What questions should the player answer?

- Who are we?
- Who rules?
- What are we trying to achieve?

Core competing pressures include:

- Growth versus stability
- Secrecy versus expansion
- Loyalty versus efficiency
- Influence versus fear
- Short-term survival versus long-term goals

Every advantage should create a vulnerability.

## Progression

### Temporal Layers

#### Static Components

Rarely change during a campaign:

- Origin
- Organization Type
- Ethos

#### Dynamic Components

Change frequently during play:

- Doctrine
- Offices
- Attributes

Most recurring decisions should emerge from dynamic components.

- Early game: establish identity and leadership.
- Mid game: expand influence and delegate power.
- Late game: manage succession, complexity, and internal pressures.

## Interactions

How does this mechanic interact with other mechanics?

- Crew occupy offices and participate in politics.
- Assets belong to and support organizations.
- Districts are administered through organizations.
- Missions advance organizational goals.
- Schemes emerge from ambitions and doctrine.

## Design Notes

### Core Gameplay Loop

Identity shapes available strategies.

Strategy creates consequences.

Consequences modify organizational state.

Changes in organizational state create leadership pressures.

Leadership changes alter future strategies.

This loop generates emergent stories and long-term organizational evolution.

### Design Principles

- Separate identity from strategy.
- Favor friction over hard restrictions.
- Prefer broad mechanics with multiple uses.
- Generate stories through systemic interactions.
- Focus on identity and governance.
- Growth creates risk.

### Failure Modes

- Character Sheet Syndrome: static choices without recurring decisions.
- Ethos Becoming Flavor: values becoming passive modifiers.
- Doctrine Becoming Solved: one optimal strategy emerging.
- Offices Becoming Busywork: administrative micromanagement.
- Internal Politics Consuming the Game: politics overshadowing the core gameplay.
- Excessive Permanence: organizations becoming unable to evolve.

### Anti-Bloat Rule

If a mechanic does not answer one of the following questions, it likely belongs elsewhere:

- Who are we?
- Who rules?
- What are we trying to achieve?
