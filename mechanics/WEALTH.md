# Wealth (Treasury)

## Purpose

Wealth represents the faction's available financial resources.

It is a core organizational resource because it reflects the faction's ability to fund operations, maintain assets, and expand its influence.

Wealth is intentionally designed to avoid becoming a universal "solve-any-problem" resource.

---

## Design Goals

- Preserve the fantasy of running a criminal organization driven by money.
- Make economic growth meaningful.
- Prevent money from becoming the dominant solution to every challenge.
- Encourage players to build networks, relationships, and assets rather than simply accumulating wealth.

---

## Generation

Wealth is primarily generated by assets and operations, such as:

- Businesses
- Protection rackets
- Smuggling routes
- Gambling dens
- Trade networks
- Investments
- Criminal enterprises

Wealth is therefore a consequence of the player's empire rather than an isolated resource generator.

---

## Spending

Wealth is used to:

- Pay salaries and upkeep
- Recruit personnel
- Establish new operations
- Expand existing assets
- Fund schemes
- Finance criminal activities
- Activate certain asset abilities

---

## Core Principle: Wealth Is Fuel, Not Capability

Wealth should rarely solve problems by itself.

Instead:

Capability + Wealth = Action

Not:

Wealth = Action

Examples:

- Reduce Heat:
  - Requires a corrupt official, judge, lawyer, or political contact.
  - Wealth funds the operation.

- Influence a vote:
  - Requires political connections.
  - Wealth funds lobbying, favors, or bribes.

- Gather information:
  - Requires informants or intelligence assets.
  - Wealth funds the investigation.

- Suppress unrest:
  - Requires enforcers, loyal lieutenants, or local authority.
  - Wealth funds the response.

---

## What Wealth Cannot Directly Do

Wealth alone should not:

- Reduce Heat
- Increase Authority
- Create loyalty
- Create political influence
- Create trusted relationships
- Instantly acquire powerful contacts

These outcomes require assets, relationships, leverage, favors, blackmail, schemes, or long-term investment.

---

## Contacts and Relationships

Important contacts are not commodities.

A corrupt judge, police chief, noble, guildmaster, or priest should not simply be purchasable through a direct payment.

Such relationships are acquired through:

- Schemes
- Favors
- Blackmail
- Shared interests
- Family ties
- Political maneuvering
- Long-term cultivation

Once acquired, Wealth may help maintain or utilize these relationships.

Wealth does not automatically create them.

---

## Strategic Consequences

A wealthy faction with poor connections may struggle to solve political or criminal problems.

A smaller faction with strong relationships, leverage, and contacts may outperform a richer rival.

This ensures that economic power remains important without overshadowing the rest of the game's systems.

---

## Player Choices and Tradeoffs

Wealth is valuable because nearly every expansion and operation requires funding, creating constant competition for limited resources.

Players must decide how to allocate their Wealth between short-term needs and long-term growth.

Examples of common tradeoffs include:

- Expanding the organization versus maintaining existing operations.
- Recruiting new personnel versus investing in businesses.
- Funding schemes versus keeping reserves for emergencies.
- Paying higher upkeep to retain valuable assets versus reducing costs.
- Investing in legitimate enterprises versus more profitable but riskier criminal ventures.

Because Wealth is fuel rather than capability, spending money alone does not guarantee success. Funding one opportunity often means delaying another.

A wealthy faction may still lack the contacts, leverage, or organizational structure required to achieve its goals, while a poorer faction with the right network may be able to exploit opportunities more effectively.

The intended tension is not "How do I spend my money?" but rather:

> "Which parts of my organization deserve funding, and which opportunities must I forgo?"

## Current Position

Wealth remains a core faction-level resource.

Its role is to fund and sustain the organization.

Its power comes from enabling assets and relationships, not replacing them.

## Open Questions

- Should Wealth be a large stockpile, a cash-flow focused resource, or a scarce resource that is frequently under pressure?
- How quickly and easily can Wealth be converted into new assets, agents, and operations?
- Should debt exist as a mechanic, and if so, how does it interact with Wealth and leverage?
- What are the consequences of running out of Wealth or being unable to cover expenses?
- Should players see a Treasury value, Income/Expenses, or both?
- How much influence should Wealth have over Authority and internal stability?
- How are important contacts maintained, and can they be lost if not properly supported?
- What are the primary Wealth sinks that prevent excessive accumulation over time?
